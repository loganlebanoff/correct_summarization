Instructions  

 Nobody actually reads those website privacy policies. You just click "I agree" and move on. Privacy policies are too long, vague, and hard to understand. The task we give here is part of a study to make privacy policies less vague. Your job is to read each of five sentence, decide how vague you believe the sentence is, and find the terms that make the sentence vague.

STEP 1: Read the sentence. Try to understand the meaning of the sentence to the best of your ability.

STEP 2: Decide how vague the sentence is.

  • Are there multiple ways of interpreting it, and you don't know which one is correct?
  • Is it unclear when, or in what cirumstances, a certain situation will occur?
  • Is there missing information that would have made the statement clearer?
  • Does the sentence have generic words/phrases (generally, usually, etc.)?

After you decide how vague the sentence is, then move the slider to match your decision. The left side of the slider indicates the sentence is clear, while the right side of the slider indicates it is more vague.

STEP 3: Find the terms that make the sentence vague

  • Some words are generic and could be replaced with a word that is clearer.
  • For example: generally, from time to time, usually, etc.
  • Carefully find these words and enter them into the box below.
  • Keep in mind that each sentence is removed from its context. Often, pronouns and proper nouns won't make sense because there is no context (for example: words like "it", "these", "they", "we", and company names like "Pintrest"). These words should NOT be considered vague and should NOT be entered into the box.
  • Separate each word with a comma ","
  • Each term should be 1 - 3 words long.

 

DON'T enter entire phrases that can be split into multiple entities. Entire phrases like this will NOT be accepted.

  • some of our customers (WRONG. "Of our customers" isn't what makes this vague. "Some" is what makes this vague.)
  • we may be (WRONG. "We" and "be" aren't what makes this vague. "May" is what makes this vague.)

DO enter short terms that makes the sentence more vague.

  • some (RIGHT. "Some" is what makes this vague.)
  • may (RIGHT. "May" is what makes this vague.)
